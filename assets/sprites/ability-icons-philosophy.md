# Tactical Sigil Geometry

## Algorithmic Philosophy

War is won in the command card. Every ability icon on the RTS ability bar is a contract between the commander and the machine -- a compressed glyph that must communicate intent, cost, and consequence in 64 square pixels. Tactical Sigil Geometry is the computational aesthetic of distilling complex agent behaviors into razor-sharp geometric compositions that read at a glance, even at 32px display scale.

The philosophy draws from military heraldry's principle of tincture -- stark contrast, no ambiguity. Each icon is constructed from layered geometric primitives: circles for containment, triangles for direction, rectangles for structure, and arcs for energy. These shapes are not decorative. They are functional glyphs that map directly to the ability's behavior. A magnifying glass means inspection. Lightning means burst power. Waves mean broadcast. The vocabulary is universal because it must be parsed in milliseconds during combat.

Color is weaponized as a three-tier hierarchy. Cyan (#00ffcc) marks the workhorses -- basic abilities available on short cooldown, the bread and butter of every engagement. Purple (#7b68ee) signals combo abilities -- utility skills that chain with basics to create compound effects. Gold (#ffd700) is reserved for ultimates -- devastating, expensive, long-cooldown abilities that reshape the battlefield. This chromatic grammar means a player never needs to read text to understand an ability's weight class. The color IS the information.

The dark field (#1a1a2e) behind each icon is not mere background. It is the void of the command center -- the deep-space black of a tactical display where every photon of light carries signal. Corner accents frame each icon like a targeting reticle, reinforcing the military-industrial aesthetic. Ultimate abilities earn an additional gold glow aura -- a visual tax on power, marking these icons as precious resources not to be spent carelessly. The inner radial gradient creates depth without dimensionality, a subtle concavity that makes each icon feel like a recessed button on a physical console.

Every line weight, every radius, every alpha value in this system was calibrated through the lens of a master interface designer who understands that game UI is not art -- it is communication under pressure. The icons must be beautiful, but their beauty is the beauty of a well-machined instrument: precise, purposeful, and lethal in its clarity.
